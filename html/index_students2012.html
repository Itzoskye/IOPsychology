2013 Students

Danielle Fox

Danielle graduated from UC Santa Cruz in 2010 with a B.A. in Psychology. As an undergraduate she participated as a Course Assistant to Introductory Psychology, worked as a research assistant studying the interaction between parent-child learning patterns, and worked as an intern for Wavestaff, an executive placement recruiting company, where she created research reports on industry trends, competitive forces, and venture capital activity in the digital media and managed services verticals to facilitate client sourcing activities. After college she worked at YouTube in a contract position as a support operator and communicated with users via email and social media to provide support for common technical problems and interface issues. At SFSU, Danielle has worked in Dr. Mosier’s lab where she has helped design a study to assess optimal collaboration strategies for asynchronous teams in high stress environments for Nasa. She also worked as a testing unit analyst in the HR department of the California Public Utilities Commission. After completing her comprehensive exam, she hopes to work in the Tech industry or Healthcare. In her spare time, she also enjoys reading, snowboarding, practicing Jujitsu, watching bad reality TV, and hanging out with her friends.

Diti Berman Harel

Diti graduated from the Tel-Aviv-Jaffa College in 2006 with a B.A. in Behavioral Sciences. Prior to her Master’s at SFSU, Diti led the eCommerce team within an Israeli CE retailer, as well as managed programs at several NGOs. Diti's experience made her fascinated with the factors that turn organizations successful: How do companies find and attract the best talent? Why do some employees love their job and will never think of leaving? Diti's passion lies in helping organizations create a healthy work environment, and applying innovative practices to increase satisfaction and performance.

Alec Munc

Alec Munc received his undergraduate degree from University of California, Santa Cruz in Psychology. His research interests are generally in Occupational Health Psychology with an emphasis on work stress and well-being. He has presented his research at such conferences as American Psychological Science, Work, Stress, & Health, and Applied Human Factors & Ergonomics. He has also interned at the Great Place to Work Institute as a Culture Audit Evaluator for both small and large organizations. He is currently pursing his PhD at Clemson University in Industrial/Organizational Psychology with an emphasis in Occupational Health.

Kendra Reich

Kendra graduated from the University of Wisconsin – Milwaukee in 2010 with a B.A. in Psychology. As an undergraduate, Kendra worked as a research assistant in the Health Psychology Lab and the Affective Neuroscience Lab. She also worked as a T.A. for the online PSYCH 101 course. During her time at SFSU, Kendra was a research assistant in Dr. Mosier’s Human Factors and Ergonomics Lab. She also interned at Pacific Gas and Electric Company in the Workforce Planning and Analytics department. Kendra hopes to pursue a career that will allow her to apply analytics towards business solutions.

Kelly Roberts

Kelly graduated from Fresno State in 2010 with a B.S. in Business Administration with an emphasis in Human Resource Management. While at SFSU, Kelly and has worked for a non-profit agency on projects pertaining to performance management, training, and selection. She also interned at Community Hospital of the Monterey Peninsula where she developed a succession management program and implementation strategy. Kelly’s research and work interests are related to selection test development, performance management, organizational culture, and job satisfaction.

Maria Sakata

Maria graduated from University of California, Irvine with a B.A. in Economics and Psychology and Social Behavior, and an M.A. in Demographics and Social Analysis. Prior to attending SFSU, she worked in education and public health research, where she conducted evaluation analysis on organizational effectiveness, as well as managed a survey research project. She completed her internship at Towers Watson, where worked on cross-functional teams to support the build and administration of engagement surveys and results reporting. For fun, she enjoys outdoor activities, playing volleyball, and picking up new hobbies such as hip hop dancing.

Chad Santos

Chad graduated from California State University, Chico in 2011 with a B.S. in Psychology and a Minor in Management of Human Resources. Upon discovering Industrial-Organizational Psychology, Chad become completely immersed in the field at SFSU and discovered a profound proclivity towards bridging the gap between research and practice to help organizations leverage their greatest assets, their people. As graduate intern with Towers Watson, a global human resources consulting firm, Chad collaborated with consultants, project team members, and various cross-functional teams to provide support on survey programming, administration, data processing, and reporting logistics needs; as well as prepared client-facing results presentations, client-reporting materials, and RFPs for Fortune 500 clients. Chad seeks to pursue a career in Human Capital consulting to effectively design and implement people solutions.

James Swarts

Before enrolling in the SFSU MS Industrial/ Organizational Psychology program, James completed a BA in Psychology at Temple University. Over the last year, James has worked on two NASA funded projects for Dr. Mosier, including the creation of a database of NextGen research and an experiment exploring asynchronous communication between remote teams. James’ first internship was in the Testing Unit of the California Public Utilities Commission as a Selections Analyst. He is currently working on the College Programs team in the Staffing Unit of Genentech, where he is contributing to college recruitment and the implementation of an extensive internship/co-op program.

Preston Treichel

Preston earned his Bachelor’s of Science from Carnegie Mellon University, graduating with an International Business major and a Spanish minor. Prior to arriving at SFSU, he spent several years in an applied setting, primarily moderating focus group discussions and consumer interviews as a qualitative market researcher. Preston has worked in Dr. Wright’s research lab at SFSU, examining candidates’ social network website profiles and their emergent implications within the employee selection process. His research interests include selection and leadership, and his master’s thesis focuses on how candidate impression management techniques influence hiring decisions. In Preston’s spare time, he enjoys catching up on critically-acclaimed films and TV programs, casual jogging during beautiful weather, sifting through any music library in hopes of discovering a new favorite band, and daydreaming about all the great things that the future holds for us.
