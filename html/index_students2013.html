2013 Students

Trevor Cherr

Trevor graduated from San Diego State University in 2011 with a B.A. in Psychology. At SDSU, he directed a research lab investigating work-family balance and supervisor support. As a Data QA Assistant at Family Health Centers of San Diego, Trevor worked and continues to work (telecommuting) on analyzing employee productivity, implementing tracking systems, and assessing organizational strengths and weaknesses. At SFSU, Trevor works in Dr. Eschleman’s research lab where he is helping to conduct a meta-analysis on employee recovery experiences outside of the workplace. He is also enrolled at Dominican University where he is earning a certificate in sustainable practices. With interests in corporate social responsibility and organizational sustainability, Trevor is currently working on a thesis investigating the effects of organizational sustainability policies on organizational attractiveness. After graduating, he hopes to help a company initiate and implement sustainability initiatives. In his spare time, Trevor enjoys spending time with his family and friends, walking, hiking, relaxing outdoors, listening to music, and watching sports and HBO.

Daniel Maurath

Daniel is a former Ohioan transplanted to the blissful bay. He received his BA in German (möchest du mal reden?) and Psychology in 2010 from Ohio State and then spent two years serving with City Year San Jose, an AmeriCorps program. Currently, he works in Dr. Mosier’s lab where he assists with NASA research that will help put a man on Mars, and interns on the data science team at a startup, Bright.com. His recovery experiences involve running some of San Francisco’s 46 hills, playing soccer, rock climbing, hiking, writing for and maintaining his website (http://www.danielmaurath.com), daydreaming about dog ownership, and investigating his capricious interests.

 

Laura Caruccie

Laura earned her Bachelor’s of Science from U.C. Davis, graduating with a degree in Psychology with a Biology emphasis. Since she started at SFSU she has worked in Dr. Mosier's Applied Psychology Lab and has been involved in a NASA funded experiment exploring asynchronous communication between remote teams. In her spare time she enjoys reading, hiking, and spending time exploring the many restaurants and attractions that San Francisco has to offer.

Athalie Tom

Athalie graduated from UC San Diego in 2012 with a B.S. in Psychology. As an undergraduate she participated in a Substance Abuse Research Lab, collecting and analyzing data on cigarette smoking and alcohol consumption in both high school and hospital settings. Interest in Consumer Psychology led her to a summer marketing internship at a local event-planning company where she researched venue availability & pricing, contacted clients for event sponsorship, and composed press releases, consumer broadcasts, and social media advertisements. Now a first-year in the I/O Psychology Master's program here at SF State, Athalie conducts literature reviews, evaluates data, and assists second-year students in their research studies as part of Dr. Chris Wright's Selection Lab. Her current interests in leadership, teamwork, and person-organization fit can be traced back to her experiences as a member and captain of the UCSD Women's Club Volleyball Team. She hopes for the opportunity to combine her love of sports with what she learns from this program in her future career.

J.R. Guzmán

Graduating from UC San Diego in 2010, J.R. double majored with a Bachelor’s of Science in Psychology and a Bachelor’s of Arts in International Studies. Due to his academic successes, undergraduate research pursuits in body image in Latino middle school students, study abroad opportunities in Chile, and on-campus involvement, J.R. was selected by the Vice Chancellor as the Outstanding Senior for the UCSD Class of 2010. Prior to arriving at SFSU, J.R. worked many years within the realm of UCSD on-campus residential life. Serving as an executive board member on residential committees, planning countless events for residents as a Resident Assistant, and working in the capacity of an Assistant Resident Dean after graduation, J.R. has vast experiences working with several diverse student populations. He decided to continue his experience within residential life at SFSU, and is currently employed as the Assistant Area Coordinator for the Towers at Centennial Square residence hall on-campus. In terms of research, J.R. enjoys topics such as group employee dynamics, employee selection, employee training, and diversity/social justice efforts relating to the workplace. He aspires to work in Human Resources and/or as a Consultant providing support/outreach to minority communities. In his spare time, you can find him listening to music, running, going to the gym, or planning fun game nights with his cohort!

Matt DeGennaro

Matt earned his Bachelor of Arts from California State University, Fullerton in 2011 with a double major in psychology and screenwriting. His prior experience includes: starting an independent record label, interning at a community mental health center, and selling women’s shoes. He has forensic research experience in assessing the effects of coaching on how to correctly feign mental illness on different psychological measures, and is currently working in Dr. Wright’s research lab as a role player for second year student Preston’s thesis on impression management and hiring decisions. Matt’s interests in I/O include: psychometrics, survey design, and workforce analytics. His favorite screenwriting style surrounds transgressive fiction.

Sean White

Sean attended Sonoma State University and earned degrees in both Psychology and Liberal Studies. After graduation, Sean spent several years working as an administrator at a Bay Area non-profit where he was responsible for the leadership of a diverse team of employees, and helped plan and execute ongoing organizational development efforts. His academic interests include the role of HR analytics in improving workforce innovation and productivity, the identification and development of peak performers, as well as a thesis exploring policy capturing of recruiters’ use of social media in employee selection. In addition to his studies at SFSU, Sean works as a Testing Unit Intern with the California Public Utilities Commission, and savors his free time hiking, cooking, and relaxing with his wife and daughter in Sonoma.

Kenson Wong

Kenson graduated from UC Davis in 2009 with a B.A. in Psychology. During his undergraduate years, his curiosity in research led him to become a research assistant for several researches. He had the opportunities to conduct interviews and develop his own survey via an internship at the Asian-American Center of Disparity. Additionally, he took a low-enrollment research course where he quantified the spatial behavior of preschool students by using helmet-mounted video cameras. Before graduating, he furthers his research experience by conducting a research during the summer at the Hong Kong University. Kenson also chose to minor in Neuroscience in order to understand the underlying neurological aspects of human perception and action. Following his graduation, Kenson became an AmeriCorps member at the Child Abuse Prevention Council where he took on the role of a parent educator. It is here where he grew accustomed to the fast-paced work environment as he facilitated multiple parenting curriculum, connected resources with families and children, and coordinated events. During his first year at SFSU, he worked in Dr. Wright’s research lab and examined the implication of certain personality in social network profiles. His current research interests include work engagement and cultural differences in organizational settings.
